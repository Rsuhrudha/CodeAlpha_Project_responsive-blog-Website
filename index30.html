Chandrayaan-1

some description

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar exploration mission launched on October 22, 2008. It discovered water molecules on the Moon's surface, indicating the presence of water ice in the polar regions. Operating for ten months, it orbited the Moon over 3,400 times before losing communication in August 2009, but its success paved the way for future Indian lunar missions.

Chandrayaan-2

some description

Chandrayaan-2 was India's second lunar mission launched on July 22, 2019. It consisted of an orbiter, a lander named Vikram, and a rover called Pragyan. The mission aimed to explore the lunar south pole region and further study water presence on the Moon's surface.

Chandrayaan-3

some description

As of my last update in September 2021, Chandrayaan-3 is the planned lunar mission by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It is intended as a follow-up to Chandrayaan-2. The mission's primary objective is to attempt a soft landing on the Moon's surface, particularly focusing on the south polar region. Further details on its status and launch date might have emerged after my last update.
